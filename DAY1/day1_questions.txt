Section 1: Basic SELECT & Column Selection (1-10)

1.Select all columns from the film table.

2.Select only title, release_year, and rental_rate from film.

3.Select first_name and last_name from the actor table.

4.Select customer_id, first_name, last_name, and email from customer.

5.Select title and description from film.

6.Select name from the category table.

7.Select address, district, and postal_code from the address table.

8.Select all columns from the staff table.

Section 2: Column Aliases (11-20)

11.Select title as "Movie Title" and rental_rate as "Rental Rate" from film.

12.Select first_name as "First Name", last_name as "Last Name" from actor.

13.Select customer_id as "ID", first_name || ' ' || last_name as "Full Name", email from customer.

14.Select title, length as "Duration in Minutes" from film.

15.Select amount as "Payment Amount", payment_date as "Paid On" from payment.

16.Select name as "Category Name" from category.

17.Select first_name as "Actor First Name", last_name as "Actor Last Name" from actor.

18.Select title as "Film", replacement_cost as "Replacement Cost (USD)" from film.

19.Select rental_date as "Rented On", return_date as "Returned On" from rental.

20.Select address as "Street Address", district as "District/Area" from address.

Section 3: DISTINCT (21-25)

22.Find all unique release_year values from film.

23.List distinct district values from the address table.

24.Find unique combinations of first_name and last_name from actor (to check for duplicate names).

25.Get distinct name from category.

28.Sort customers by last_name ascending, then first_name ascending.

29.Sort payments by amount descending.

30.Sort actors by last_name descending.

31.List films sorted by length ascending (shortest first).

32.Sort films by replacement_cost descending, then by rental_rate ascending.

33.List all cities sorted by city name.

34.Sort rentals by rental_date descending (most recent first).

35.Sort staff by last_name.
